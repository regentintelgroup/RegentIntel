    IRC §132 Security Study & Assessment

    The security foundation your tax position requires.

    A properly structured executive security program may qualify as a working condition fringe benefit under IRC §132(d), excluding the value from the executive's taxable income. The tax position rests on a documented security foundation. Regent Intel provides that foundation.

    The Assessment

    Security necessity, documented to standard.

    Regent Intel conducts a formal Security Study and Assessment establishing the business necessity of protective measures for the subject executive. The assessment evaluates threat exposure, identifies specific risk vectors, and documents the factual basis for a security program in a format structured for use by the company's tax and legal counsel.

    We do not provide tax advice. We provide the security analysis the tax position rests on — produced by licensed practitioners, documented to evidentiary standard, and defensible under IRS scrutiny.

    Assessment

    Threat & Exposure Evaluation

    Structured evaluation of the executive's personal threat environment — visibility, position, industry exposure, travel patterns, public profile, and specific risk indicators relevant to their circumstances.

    Documentation

    Governance-Grade Reporting

    Findings documented in a format designed for executive review, legal scrutiny, and tax counsel use. Structured to support the company's §132 position without overstating or understating the security picture.

    Alignment

    Program Recommendation

    Clear articulation of what protective measures are warranted by the documented threat picture — supporting proportionality, which is a key element of the §132 analysis.

    Support

    Counsel Coordination

    Available to work directly with the company's tax counsel, HR leadership, and board advisors to ensure the security assessment integrates cleanly with their §132 analysis and documentation.

    Who This Serves

    Corporate boards. Tax counsel. Compensation committees.

    The IRC §132 Security Study is a standalone engagement — not a prerequisite for a protection program. It is appropriate for organizations where an executive protection program is already in place or under consideration, and where the company's tax and legal advisors need a documented security foundation.
